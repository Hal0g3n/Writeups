Problem Setup

Basically, that 170 lines of super extra and useless code overloads our brain with 2 sets of 128 different signatures, 8 "sigma" signatures and 120 theta signatures. They are defined as follows:

\[ \begin{aligned} p &\text{ is prime} \\ q &\text{ is prime} \\ N &= p * q \\ \sigma_i &= m_i^{-N}\ &\mod{N}& \text{, where}\ 0 \leq i < 8 \\ \theta_i &= \text{crt}(\pm\sqrt{m_i} \mod p,\ \sqrt{m_i} \mod q)\ &\mod{pq}& \text{, where}\ 8 \leq i < 128 \end{aligned} \]

The sign of \(\pm\sqrt{m_i} \mod p\) used to calculate \(\theta_i\) is randomised using python's random.random() at \(50\%\) chance.

Honestly, idk why so many numbers were given.

Solution

This challenge is centered around 1 observation, the fact that we are given both signs of \(\pm\sqrt{m_i} \mod p\ \exists\ 8 \leq i < 128\). Why do we have both signs well...

Extra Math

For each "theta" message, we generate 2 "theta" signatures due to generating them twice. This means that the chance of both signatures message having the same sign is \(50\%\).

For all 120 messages to have the same sign, the chance is:

\[50\% ^ {120} = 7.5231638 \times 10^{-35}\]

That means that the chance of never knowing both \(\pm\sqrt{m_i} \mod p\) for all 120 messages is less likely than you getting struck by meteorite, but still more likely than me getting a girlfriend 😔

Anyways, we can also just look at the numbers given and see that they are indeed different so this proof is just like this problem, super extra.

The observation

Let us define the 2 values of \(\theta_i\) as \(\theta_i^+\) and \(\theta_i^-\), where the sign represents the sign on \(\pm\sqrt{m_i} \mod p\).

\[ \begin{aligned} \sqrt{m_i} &= \theta_i^+ \mod p\\ -\sqrt{m_i} &= \theta_i^- \mod p \end{aligned} \]

The magic trick is to just sum them and we get

\[ \begin{aligned} \theta_i^+ + \theta_i^- &= \sqrt{m_i} -\sqrt{m_i} \mod p\\ &= 0 \mod p \\ &= kp, \text{for some $k\in\mathbb{Z}$} \end{aligned} \]

Now, we just \(\gcd(\theta_i^+ + \theta_i^-, N) = \gcd(kp, pq) = p\).

And now with 1 factor known we just do as per normal RSA and plug it into my favourite sagemath dcode.fr
